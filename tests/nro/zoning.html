Hillsborough County uses various means to notify the public of upcoming Public Hearings and recognizes that neighborhoods and civic associations want to participate in planning and development issues such as:

  • Requests for rezoning
  • Minor and Major Modifications to existing Planned Development zoning districts and Special Use Permits
  • Special Use Permit requests for items such as liquor stores and solid waste (trash) facilities


To provide neighborhood groups with a greater opportunity to participate in the decisions which affect their residents, the Board of County Commissioners approved procedures that interested neighborhoods within one mile of a proposed development activity be notified. Notices concerning applications and dates for public hearings are sent by mail.

Organizations which represent a geographic area of no less than 50 households, and which actually represent 50 percent of those households (no less than 25 households) may register for these zoning notifications.
