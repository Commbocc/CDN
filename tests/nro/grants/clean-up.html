This grant is administered through the Office of Neighborhood Relations in collaboration with the Public Utilities Department. This program is only for associations in Unincorporated Hillsborough County. Applications will be accepted on a continuous basis and evaluated for eligibility and allocation as received until available funds have been depleted. Only one application per association per fiscal year will be considered. The maximum individual award will be for 5 roll-off dumpsters.

Program Purpose

The broad purpose of the program is to involve citizens in their community upkeep, retain property values within neighborhoods, and reduce factors that contribute to crime. The specific purpose of this program is to encourage members of neighborhood associations to conduct clean-up projects in their own community, which can include litter pick up, yard waste, appliances, metal, furniture, and wood for deposit in roll-off containers.


Projects For Consideration

Neighborhood leaders are invited to propose projects that will clean up the area within their boundaries which have become littered or unsightly, and provide residents an opportunity to dispose of large household items not eligible for normal trash pick-up. Labor must be provided by neighborhood residents as an in-kind contribution to the proposed project. No pond clean-up applications will be approved under this program.


Who Can Apply

Neighborhood, Civic, Homeowner Associations, Crime Watch Programs and Special Taxing Districts in Unincorporated Hillsborough County are eligible to apply. A specific area that the applicant represents must be identified and evidence of active local resident involvement and concurrence will be required. For-profit service providers and local government entitles are not eligible to apply.

Additional Information

Waste Removal - The association will be responsible for providing Neighborhood Relations 30 days prior notice of the date that a clean-up will be held. The County has a contract with Republic Services, Inc. to provide a maximum of five 30-yard roll-off containers on any given Saturday, with only One (1) clean-up per Saturday. When the date has been established, the Neighborhood Relations Liaison will coordinate with Hillsborough County Public Utilities Department. The Hillsborough County Public Utilities Department will be responsible for contacting Republic Services, Inc., directly for the roll-off containers (dumpsters) to be delivered to the physical location identified by the association. Associations are not to pay the company directly. Payments for roll-offs will be made by the Public Utilities Department to the company.

All items must be separated by type and placed into separate roll-offs. Yard waste can be disposed of if a separate container is ordered for them. Appliances (also known as white goods) and other Clean-up Grant - FY 2015 metal items should go in one roll-off. Appliances containing Freon should either be in a separate container, or grouped in one area of the appliance container.

What will be accepted at the roll-offs:
  • Old refrigerators, ovens, water heaters, steel and other metals, old furniture (such as couches, chairs, tables and mattresses), construction debris (such as concrete, bricks, glass and roofing materials), and tires.
  • No items normally picked up at the curb by Hillsborough County (household trash), solid waste from commercial activity, or hazardous waste such as paint, household chemicals, batteries and propane tanks.
  • NO PROFESSIONAL SERVICES OR SUB-CONTRACTORS, STRICTLY FOR HOMEOWNERS.
  • If depositing yard waste, that waste must be placed in a separate roll-off container. The waste cannot be in plastic bags, because the bags are not processable. All bags containing leaves, tree cuttings, twigs, etc., must be emptied into the RO and the bags kept by the residents and placed in their regular household garbage container for collection.
  • Since the County's disposal sites will not accept yard waste ROs with bags, you need to ensure that all participants are aware of this process. Otherwise, pick-up of the roll-off will be refused or it will be returned to the neighborhood for sorting and removal of the bags, making a lot of extra work for everyone.
  • All tires must be placed in the same roll-off(s).
  • No televisions, microwaves, computers or radios are allowed in the roll-off containers.

Should you have any questions about these rules, please feel free to contact Patti Conover of the Hillsborough County at 272-5977 extension 43924.

The Public Utilities Department is responsible for ordering the roll-off containers directly from Republic Services, Inc.

The invoices for roll-off service should be directed to the Hillsborough County Public Utilities Department for payment. If you receive an invoice, do not pay it. Forward it to your coordinator at ONR.

The Hillsborough County Public Utilities Department will forward Disposal Waiver Letters to RepublicServices, Inc., for the waiving of disposal fees at the County's facilities.
