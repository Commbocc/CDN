Grants at a Glance

Each year Neighborhood Relations awards XXX in grants to eligible neighborhoods and organizations. See a full list of grants below.

Grant Name Purpose Eligibility Details
Neighborhood Mini Grant This grant is to support activities that improve communities, strengthen neighborhood associations, and encourage civic involvement. Neighborhood, Civic, Homeowner Associations, Crime Watch Programs and Special Taxing Districts in Unincorporated Hillsborough County are eligible to apply
Amount Funded
$2,500 per association. Maximum
Deadline
Aug. 7, 2015
How to Apply?
N/A
Community Clean Up Mini-Grant This grant assist citizens in community upkeep to retain property values and reduce crime. Successful projects include litter pick up and yard waste removal. Neighborhood, Civic, Homeowner Associations, Crime Watch Programs and Special Taxing Districts in Unincorporated Hillsborough County are eligible to apply
Amount Funded
N/A
Deadline
N/A

Low Volume Irrigation Mini-Grant This grant funds low-volume irrigation systems in community-maintained areas to conserve water. Funds may be used to retrofit current systems, or install new systems in areas with a readily available water hook-up. Neighborhood, Civic, Homeowner Associations and Special Taxing Districts in Unincorporated Hillsborough County are eligible to apply for funding.
Amount Funded
N/A
Deadline
N/A
How to Apply?

Tree Program Mini-Grant This is a matching grant program to plant trees in public areas, private common areas, and especially in community-maintained areas and road rights-of-way within or connected to the neighborhood. Neighborhood, Civic, Homeowner Associations, Community Development Corporations and Special Taxing Districts located in Unincorporated Hillsborough County are eligible to apply for funding. Homeowner Associations must be released from their developer.
Amount Funded
Matching awards of $2,500 per neighborhood association
