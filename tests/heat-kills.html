Heat Kills!

Do NOT leave a dog or cat in a closed vehicle.
IT'S AGAINST THE LAW.

The inside of cars and trucks can heat up very quickly, even with windows partially open. When the temperature outside is 65°, the interior of a vehicle can reach 102° within 10 minutes, and 120° within 30 minutes! Florida's year-round heat and humidity increase the danger. Pets left inside closed vehicles can quickly develop heatstroke, and it's often lethal.

Symptoms of Heatstroke:

  • Excessive panting and drooling
  • Disorientation
  • Weakness
  • Seizures

First Aid:

  • Lower the body temperature by submerging in water or running a hose over the animal.

    NOTE: It is very important to cool the animal's head. Put the animal in an air-conditioned vehicle, building or at least in the shade.

  • Call a veterinarian immediately.
  • Notify authorities.
