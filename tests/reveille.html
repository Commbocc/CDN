As an on-going effort to answer the national call to end Veteran homelessness by 2015, the Tampa-Hillsborough County Continuum of Care (CoC), as part of the 25 Cities Initiative and the Mayor's Challenge to end Veteran homelessness, is working diligently to bring our community to "Functional Zero". This multi-sector collaboration will incorporate federal, regional, state and local government agencies, faith-based organizations, housing providers, non-profit agencies and businesses to connect 50 homeless Veterans to permanent or permanent supportive housing on that day. Other services, such as intensive case management, employment services, legal assistance, mental health services, benefits eligibility screenings, Veterans benefits, financial counseling, health care, and household supplies will also be provided.

GOAL: End Homelessness for All Veterans

  1. Rapidly re-housing 50 homeless Veterans by Veterans' Day 2015.
  2. Bringing together a wide range of partners to ensure a collaborative impact on a national level.
  3. Developing a system of service that ensures that a Veteran never sleeps on the streets or in an emergency shelter in Hillsborough County.

Eligibility (An honorable discharge status is NOT required)

Persons who have served in the military and are currently sleeping on the streets, in an emergency shelter or a place not meant for human habitation. Transitional housing residents are not eligible for Operation: REVEILLE.
