Hillsborough County's Social Services department administers two federally funded energy assistance programs to households in need. The program supports the County's vision of residents living safe, healthy and self-reliant lives.

Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program

Who is Eligible?

Households must meet the following requirements:

  1. Have a total household gross income of no more than 150 percent of the Federal Poverty guidelines.
  2. Be responsible for paying their home energy bills.
  3. Be a resident of Hillsborough County.
  4. Be a U.S. Citizen, qualified alien, or permanent resident of the U.S.

  • Additional requirements may be necessary to determine eligibility. Applicants will be informed at the time of application.

Emergency Home Energy Assistance Program

Who is Eligible?

Households must meet the following requirements:

  1. Have a total household gross income of no more than 150 percent of the Federal Poverty guidelines.
  2. At least one household member must be 60 years of age or older.
  3. Be responsible for paying their home energy bills.
  4. Be a resident of Hillsborough County.
  5. Be a U.S. Citizen, qualified alien, or permanent resident of the U.S.
  6. Must have a recent past due or final notice energy bill.

  • They receive a notice indicating their electric bill is delinquent or past due;
  • They have been notified that their electricity is going to be cut off;
  • Their electricity has been cut off; or
  • They are unable to get delivery of fuel, are out of fuel, or are in danger of running out of fuel.
  • Additional requirements may be necessary to determine eligibility. Applicants will be informed of these requirements at the time of application.

How To Apply for Low Income Energy Assistance or Emergency Home Energy Assistance

Applications are accepted at a local Community Resource Center. When applying for assistance, you must bring:

  1. Proof of income for all household members for the last 90 days such as: (pay check stub, employer letter, award letter for public assistance, Social Security Income verification, unemployment benefit letter, or pension check stub.)
  2. Proof of home energy obligation.
  3. Social Security Number verification for each household member.
  4. Picture identification for all adults in the household.
  5. Identification for all other household members.
  6. Two items showing proof of Hillsborough County residency:

  • Valid driver's license with current address,
  • Letter or current lease from landlord or mortgage company,
  • Hillsborough County voter registration card
  • Public utility bill with Hillsborough County address
  • Vehicle registration with Hillsborough County address
  • Current public school enrollment in Hillsborough County
  • Written verification from another community agency
