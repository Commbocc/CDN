Understanding the Budget

Our Residents' Guide to How the Budget Works & Where the Money Goes

The budget process for a large local government like Hillsborough County has a lot of moving parts. Money flows in from various places, including your tax dollars, and is divided among constitutionally-mandated offices like Tax Collector, Sheriff's Office, Board of County Commissioners, etc.

Not only that, but money that comes from certain sources (i.e. federal grants, specific tax initiatives) has restrictions on how it can be spent. There are strings attached that must be accounted for!

That said, there are 3 steps to understanding where our budget comes from, where it goes, and how we spend it.

WHERE THE MONEY COMES FROM

The County's budget for FY 2016/2017 is $4.72 Billion, but it's helpful to note where that number comes from. It includes non-cash sources (like reserves and transfers) that cannot be spent. The cash budget (a.k.a. what we really have to work with) comes to $1.83 billion.

WHERE THE MONEY GOES

Hillsborough County consists of 9 constitutionally mandated offices, and that $1.83 billion cash budget is divided between them. After all is said and done, the County Administrator's share of that budget is $376.1 million, which is further divided among our many departments.
